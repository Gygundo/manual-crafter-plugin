# Encounter Church — Default Voice Profile

> Default voice profile for Encounter Church (encounterchurch.co.za), South Africa.
> Based on the senior pastor's preaching and teaching voice.

## 1. Tone

Bold, direct, and pastorally warm. Speaks with apostolic conviction — statements land as settled truth, not tentative suggestions. Accessible without being shallow. The voice of a father who believes deeply and wants those he leads to believe the same. South African context is present but never parochial.

## 2. Sentence Patterns

- Mix of medium sentences (15-25 words) and short punchy statements (5-10 words) for emphasis
- Rhetorical questions used sparingly but powerfully — typically to surface what the reader already feels before providing the answer
- Fragments acceptable for emphasis: "That is the Kingdom." "Not religion. Reality."
- Repetition used for theological anchoring: restating a key truth 2-3 times across a section in different words
- Paragraphs are short (3-5 sentences) — each advancing one idea before moving on

## 3. Vocabulary

### Use
- "Kingdom" (not "Christian life" or "faith journey")
- "Supernatural" as normative, not exceptional
- "Believer" rather than "Christian" for active disciples
- "Revelation" for spiritual insight received, not just Bible knowledge
- "Walk" and "lifestyle" for the lived experience of faith
- "Foundation" and "establish" for doctrinal grounding
- "Encounter" as a verb — believers encounter God, not merely experience Him

### Avoid
- Academic or seminary vocabulary (soteriology, pneumatology, eschatology as standalone terms)
- Passive constructions that remove the believer's agency ("it is hoped that...")
- Hedging language ("perhaps", "it might be said", "in some ways")
- Generalised Christian clichés ("walk with God", "let go and let God", "blessed")
- American evangelical idiom (altar call language, revival clichés)

## 4. Emphasis Techniques

- **Short declarative standalone sentences** after a longer explanation: sets the truth as a capstone
- **Scripture block followed by immediate application** — never let a quoted verse sit without unpacking
- **Contrast structures** — "Not X. Y." — used to strip away wrong thinking and replace it
- **Repetition with escalation** — a key phrase repeated 2-3 times, each time adding dimension

## 5. Anti-Patterns (Never Do This)

- Never soften a doctrinal position with "of course, there are different views"
- Never use therapeutic language as the primary frame (healing, wellness, wholeness as endpoints rather than byproducts of Kingdom living)
- Never write apologetically about the supernatural — miracles are assumed, not defended
- Never pad sections with inspirational filler — every paragraph must carry theological weight
- Never end a section with a vague encouragement — end with a declaration or a call to action

## 6. Theological Framework

Grace-based but not passive. The New Covenant establishes believers as sons and daughters with full access to the Father — this is the starting point, not the reward. Kingdom authority is the believer's inheritance, not a special gift for the few. The supernatural (healing, deliverance, miracles) is normative church life, not exceptional. The apostolic and prophetic are active governmental gifts in the church today. Obedience is the natural response to revelation, not a means of earning favour.

## 7. Scripture Handling

- Default translation: NKJV unless source material uses another consistently
- Scripture quoted in full, then referenced in parentheses: *"For God so loved the world..."* (John 3:16)
- Never paraphrase scripture as if it is a direct quote — clearly distinguish
- Multiple scriptures on a theme: listed as a block, not embedded mid-sentence
- South African English spelling throughout (honour, favour, recognise)
